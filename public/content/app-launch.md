[section: A Long Pause]
It has been a long time since I last wrote a blog here (most of my previous posts were deleted occasionally, I will say why). A lot has happened in between - life really did feel like a roller coaster.
 
There were many times when I found it hard to write, even though writing is something I deeply love. It has always been an important part of my life. Whenever I feel low, I usually do two things: read my old journals or write a new one with listening songs. Those journals hold a lot of my emotions.

Every time I read my past writing, my entire perspective shifts. It may sound a bit philosophical, but I feel that the lower we feel, the narrower our thinking becomes. We slowly drown in our own emotions. Humans need distractions, I have a lot which i will share lately. But journaling is a special one for me, revisiting my past moments and thoughts somehow pulls me out of that state.

At some point, I wanted to write on a platform where others could also read my thoughts. So I started publishing blogs. But // silly me //  wanted everything to be perfect. I began using AI heavily to rephrase my writing and make it sound more “expert.”

After a while, something felt off.

The journal in my diary and the blog I published started to feel like two different people. The purpose of the page was to reflect me, but instead it felt… refracted. That's why I deleted a lot of blogs I wrote previously.

At the same time, I genuinely wanted to improve my writing from the audience’s perspective. So I tried reducing AI usage. Still, managing everything became difficult. I kept redrafting, asking AI for feedback and suggestions, trying to use it more like a mentor than a ghostwriter.

There was lot of friction in that way of learning. Eventually, I lost motivation and stopped writing.

All I really wanted was a tool that could guide me to write better without taking control away from me.

So I built one.

I wanted something that supports the way I actually write:

1. Suggestions & Feedback
I often ask AI for feedback because it helps me improve while keeping my own voice. I don’t want full rewrites instead I want direction.

2. Light Rephrase
Sometimes I just want to see a clearer way to express the same idea. Not a complete rewrite, just gentle improvement or simplification.

3. Finding Better Words (will add soon)
Not fancy vocabulary — just the right words that help me articulate more precisely.

So I started building Verbo, with the hope of learning writing more effectively.

Right now, the first two features are live, and I have many more ideas planned. I’m sharing this early version to get feedback from people who genuinely care about improving their writing.

Honestly, I’m not even sure if I would have published this. I’ve rarely shared my blogs openly before. But I’m trying to become a little more extroverted.

This post itself is written using the same tool.

I’m planning to write more consistently from now on.

Fingers crossed.
